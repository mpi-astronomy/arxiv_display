# 5. Star-Planet Interactions: The Instrumentation Perspective 

</div>
<div id="comments">

[![arXiv](https://img.shields.io/badge/arXiv-2607.04874-b31b1b.svg)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04874)<mark>Appeared on: 2026-07-07</mark> -  _Submitted to Space Science Reviews. Comments welcome_

</div>
<div id="authors">

H. a. H. K. e. korhonen@mpia.de}, et al.

</div>
<div id="abstract">

**Abstract:** $\noindent$ Star-Planet Interactions (SPIs) produce observable phenomena across the electromagnetic spectrum, from X-ray and ultraviolet emission tracing magnetic activity and atmospheric escape to optical, infrared, and radio signatures probing stellar variability, planetary atmospheres, and magnetospheric interactions. This chapter reviews the current observational capabilities for SPI research, focusing on the instruments and facilities currently used, or technically capable of being used, for SPI studies. Particular emphasis is placed on the instrumental characteristics required to detect often weak and transient SPI signatures, as well as on the observational challenges associated with such measurements. The chapter concludes with an outlook on upcoming facilities that are expected to enhance our ability to detect, characterise, and understand SPI phenomena in the coming years.
